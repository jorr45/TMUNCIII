POLICIES

Registration

Space is limited, so registration may close once all spots have been taken.

Early registration ends December 19th.
The deadline to register for the conference is February 13th, which is also the deadline to change delegation size.
(Trinity delegates may register until February 20th.)

Early Registration: $20 per delegate plus $30 school fee
Regular Registration: $25 per delegate plus $40 school fee

Priority country selection will be granted to delegations on a first come, first served basis in the SOCHUM and UNSC committees. Priority selection will not be observed in the Irish Home Rule, Fall of Rome, or Ministry of Magic committees.

Early registrants will receive their committee positions by the end of December. Regular registrants will be processed on a rolling basis.

Pre-written Material

Any committee legislation that is submitted for committee and/or dais consideration or discussed with other delegates must be written after the start of Committee Session I. Such legislation includes, but is not limited to: working papers, draft resolutions, directives, communiques, press releases, and war plans. Any delegate found to have pre-written legislation will be disqualified from receiving awards, and his or her team disqualified from receiving a delegation award.

Lunch

TMUNC will provide pizza for lunch to all delegates. Delegates may also leave the building to purchase their own lunch, with their advisor's permission.

Laptops

Laptop use is permitted outside the committee room for the General Assembly committee (though handwritten legislation will still be accepted). Trinity will provide delegates with laptops in the Ministry of Magic crisis committee. Delegates are expected to use electronic devices appropriately at all times; laptop use while committee is in session must be for committee purposes only.

Awards

The dais on each committee will determine individual awards for delegates who are most effective in committee. Delegates who submit position papers via email to committee chairs by February 21st will be eligible for a position paper award. Delegations will receive awards on a point-based system, with one award for the Best Delegation.

Plagiarism

All committee legislation submitted for consideration to the committee and/or dais must be the original work of the sponsors. Any delegate found to have submitted plagiarized material will be disqualified from receiving awards, and his or her team disqualified from receiving a delegation award.

Position Papers

While not required, position papers are highly encouraged and should be submitted to committee chairs by Saturday, February 21st. Position papers should be limited to one page, single-spaced, include the delegate's committee and country at the top, and include footnoted or endnoted source citation (citations do not count toward the page limit).
